# Real Value Economics

## Definition of K

K is defined as 1 Megawatt-hour (1 MWh) of effective productive energy.

K represents a measurable physical unit of energy used in productive transformation.

---

## Productive Transformation Coefficient α

Every productive process contains two measurable physical determinants:

- Energy expenditure (E)  
- Wear and degradation of productive means (W)

Human labour does not constitute a thermodynamic input equivalent to energy or wear.  
It is the necessary structural condition for the activation and coordination of productive transformation.

Raw materials do not constitute an independent physical determinant. Their contribution is embodied in previously expended energy and wear.

Time is not a productive resource. It is the structural dimension within which transformation unfolds.

The productive coefficient α aggregates the measurable physical load:

α = f(E, W)

α is not chosen, negotiated, or priced.  
It emerges from the physical structure of production.

---

## Physical Interpretation

Coefficient α expresses the physical load associated with producing a reference quantity of output.

It does not describe monetary valuation.  
It describes the real physical demand imposed on the productive base.

Processes with higher α require greater physical load.  
Processes with lower α achieve the same output with lighter physical demand.

---

# Structural Levels of the K Framework

The K framework operates across three structurally distinct levels.  
The strict separation of these levels preserves scientific objectivity.

---

## Level I — Objective Physical Valuation

At the foundational level, production is measured exclusively through physically measurable magnitudes.

α = f(E, W)

The objective physical cost expressed in K is:

K_cost = α

This level contains no pricing and no distribution.

---

## Level II — Structural Pricing

Production cannot circulate without economic coordination.

K_price = K_cost + ΔK

ΔK represents the structural coordination differential required for:

- Labour remuneration  
- Entrepreneurial initiative  
- Capital maintenance  
- Risk absorption  
- Institutional continuity  

ΔK emerges from economic organization and market dynamics.  
It is bounded by physical cost but not reducible to it.

---

## Level III — Distribution and Fiscal Structure

Distribution determines the allocation of ΔK.

ΔK = Wages + Profit + Tax

At this level:

- Labour is compensated in K.
- Capital receives return.
- The state may collect tax in K.
- Redistribution may operate.

Tax does not alter physical cost.  
It reallocates part of ΔK within the economic structure.

---

## Structural Integrity

The separation between:

1. Physical valuation  
2. Structural pricing  
3. Distribution  

ensures:

- Measurement remains objective.
- Labour is not reduced to a thermodynamic magnitude.
- Justice operates at the distributional level.
- The framework remains adaptable under technological transformation, including automation.
